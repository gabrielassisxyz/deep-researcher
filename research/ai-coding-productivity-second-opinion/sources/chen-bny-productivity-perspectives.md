# Chen et al. BNY Mellon Productivity Perspectives

## Source Type

arXiv preprint based on mixed-method research at BNY Mellon. It is not direct controlled speed evidence.

## Method

Survey with 2,989 developer responses and 11 interviews.

## Key Evidence

- Survey results expose conflicting views of AI coding assistant usefulness.
- Interviews identify short-term and long-term productivity factors, including technical expertise and ownership of work.
- The paper argues that AI productivity evaluation needs multiple dimensions beyond commit metrics.

## Caveats

The study is mainly perception and measurement-framework evidence, not measured speed. It is useful for explaining why surveys and metrics can diverge, not for proving acceleration.

## Practical Implication

Self-reported productivity is multidimensional and can reflect flow, reduced frustration, or ownership effects rather than faster delivery.
