# Stray et al. NAV Copilot Longitudinal Case Study

## Source Type

arXiv preprint / longitudinal mixed-methods case study in a large public-sector agile organization.

## Method

Analysis of 26,317 non-merge commits from 703 GitHub repositories over two years, focusing on 25 Copilot users and 14 non-users, plus survey responses and 13 interviews.

## Key Evidence

- Copilot users were already more active than non-users before adoption, showing self-selection.
- The study did not find statistically significant changes in commit-based activity after Copilot adoption, though minor increases appeared.
- Perceived productivity had negligible correlation with commit metrics.
- Some developers felt more productive even when average weekly activity declined.
- The study reports no measured negative impact on structural code-quality metrics.

## Caveats

Commit metrics are imperfect productivity proxies. Sample size is modest. It is a case study, not a randomized experiment.

## Practical Implication

This is direct evidence that subjective productivity and measured activity can diverge in real organizational work.
