# Anthropic Skill Formation RCT

## Source Type

Vendor-authored research article with a linked paper. Anthropic is a vendor of AI assistants, so performance claims should not exceed medium confidence without independent replication.

## Method

Randomized controlled trial with software developers learning and using a new Python library, with or without AI assistance.

## Key Evidence

- Anthropic reports AI assistance caused a statistically significant decrease in mastery.
- Participants in the AI group scored 17% lower on a follow-up quiz covering concepts they had just used.
- AI sped up the task slightly, but the speed gain was not statistically significant.
- The article frames the issue as cognitive offloading: humans still need skills to catch errors and guide AI output.

## Caveats

The setting is learning a new library, not experienced developers in a familiar codebase. The source is vendor-authored.

## Practical Implication

AI can shift costs from initial implementation time to comprehension and oversight. This matters for maintenance, onboarding, and review burden even when code appears faster to produce.
