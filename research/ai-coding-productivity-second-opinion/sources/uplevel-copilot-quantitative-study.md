# Uplevel Copilot Quantitative Study

## Source Type

Vendor-authored report by a software engineering intelligence company. It is a primary source for its own dataset and marketing about its own analysis.

## Method

Uplevel reports analyzing engineering data from nearly 800 developers using objective metrics including cycle time, PR throughput, bug rate, and extended working hours.

## Key Evidence

- Uplevel reports little to no productivity gain from GitHub Copilot access.
- It reports no significant change in efficiency metrics.
- It reports the Copilot group introduced 41% more bugs.

## Caveats

The source is vendor-authored, and the public blog is not a peer-reviewed paper. Details may be less transparent than academic studies. It should not be treated as high-confidence causal evidence.

## Practical Implication

Useful contradictory evidence: measured engineering metrics may fail to improve even when developers feel AI helps, and defect burden may rise.
