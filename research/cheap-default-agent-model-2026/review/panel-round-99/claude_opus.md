# Review — claude:opus — Round 99

The dossier is well-structured, honest about the fit question, and disciplined about claim
typing in `evidence.md` — Gate 4.5 passes on its face (no vendor performance claim at `high`,
no fit claim above `low`). The problems are underneath that surface. Two are structural: the
gap loop marked eight of eight gaps `blocked` while leaving four rounds and thirty-two sources
of budget unspent, and the recommended model's identity was never actually pinned down. The
rest are citations that do not survive being opened, and arithmetic that does not survive being
redone.

## False-Positive Risks

Ranked by how much I doubt them.

- **F11 is the one I am least sure of.** The source notes are explicitly headed "Key claims used" — they are summaries, not transcripts. The Grok and Codestral figures may well be on the live pages and simply never made it into the notes. I flagged it anyway because the dossier's auditability depends on the notes being sufficient, but "the note is incomplete" is a materially lighter charge than "the number was invented," and I cannot distinguish them without re-fetching the pages. Treat this as a note-completeness finding, not an accusation.
- **F8 rests on my knowledge that openlm.ai is not Z.ai's domain.** I did not fetch either site. If openlm.ai is republishing Z.ai's release announcement verbatim, the *facts* are probably fine and only the `source_type`/`publisher`/`confidence` labels are wrong. The label error is still real, but the downstream damage may be nil.
- **F2's severity depends on a fact I could not verify.** If "V4-Flash-Max" turns out to be llm-stats' cosmetic rendering of `deepseek-v4-flash`, this collapses to a minor naming nit. I rate it critical because the dossier *itself* raised the discrepancy (M1), noticed it "may be a different sub-variant," and then closed it as a price question without checking — and because the cost of being wrong is that the recommendation's evidence base disappears. The asymmetry, not my certainty, is what makes it critical.
- **F1 assumes the unscraped URLs would actually have yielded something.** I did not fetch tbench.ai or the OpenRouter MiniMax page to confirm. But the finding does not depend on that: the defect is that `blocked` was asserted without trying, and the dossier admits as much in its own words. Even if every fetch came back empty, the correct process was to fetch and *then* block.
- **F4, F5, F6, F7, F10 I am confident in.** They are arithmetic redone from the dossier's own numbers, or a grep against the dossier's own source notes. F4's three bad rows and F10's inconsistency reproduce on a calculator; F5's $1.74/$3.48, F6's 60–80%, and F7's "official" appear in no source note in the directory.

## What The Dossier Got Right

Not politeness — these are real, and several are things most dossiers fail at.

- **The fit question is handled exactly as the contract demanded.** It is named as inherently unanswerable, placed in the headline rather than a footnote, capped at `low`, and — the part that actually matters — converted into a concrete, runnable experiment with a task set, a scoring rubric, a run count, a variance threshold, and a stated decision rule ("within ~5 points → switch; >15 points worse → don't"). That decision rule is the single most useful artifact here.
- **Claim typing is genuinely enforced, not decorated.** `evidence.md` separates Facts / Performance / Fit into three tables with different ceilings, and every vendor performance claim (GLM-5.2's Terminal-Bench 81.0, MiniMax's demos, DeepSeek's ~79%) is capped at `medium`. Gate 4.5 passes.
- **The search ledger is real.** Twenty-one queries, fruitless ones logged with what junk they returned ("Society of Women Engineers", the senior-living careers page), sources traced to the query that surfaced them, and a rejected-sources list with reasons. This is exactly what the ledger is for — and it is *because* the ledger is honest that F1 was findable at all. The dossier caught itself; it just did not act on it.
- **The candidate set was genuinely discovered.** The prompt named zero models, and the shortlist (DeepSeek V4-Flash, MiniMax M3, GLM-5.2, plus a ten-model rejected set) is traceable to the llm-stats leaderboard scrape, not to memory.
- **The main cost model is correct.** I recomputed every cell of both volume scenarios, the 80/20 blend, and the cache-hit case: all check out. The errors are in the *secondary* table (F4), not the parametric model the user will actually plug their numbers into.
- **The strongest risk signal is not buried.** DeepSeek's "High — variable availability" / "Medium — check status" rating is surfaced in synthesis, in the model note, and turned into a concrete LiteLLM fallback chain — rather than being softened because it complicates the recommendation.
