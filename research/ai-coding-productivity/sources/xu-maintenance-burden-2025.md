# Xu et al. — Maintenance Burden on Experienced Developers

**Found by the codex second opinion (Gate 8.4); added to this dossier after
reconciliation.** Independent arXiv working paper. **Directly on the user's condition**
(experienced developers) — the most on-point non-speed source in the dossier.

## Key findings (per second-opinion capture)

- AI-assisted programming **decreases the productivity of experienced developers** by
  increasing technical debt and maintenance burden.
- Aggregate productivity gains are **driven by peripheral developers**; **core/experienced
  developers carry more review and rework burden** and lose original-code productivity.
- I.e. the headline aggregate can look positive while the experienced-developer slice is
  negative — a within-study version of the local-vs-systemic divergence.

## Why this matters

- This is the cleanest single-study articulation of *why* AI can slow experienced
  developers even when org-level metrics look positive: the maintenance/review burden
  shifts onto them.
- Corroborates METR (experienced-dev slowdown), DORA (stability decline), and
  BNY Mellon (long-term costs undercounted) from a different angle.
- Caveat: arXiv working paper; treat as independent preprint evidence pending peer review.
  Confidence medium.
