# METR Early-2026 Self-Report Survey

Survey (not RCT) of 349 technical workers (87 SWEs, 71 researchers, 129 academics/PhDs, 48
founders/managers). Feb–April 2026. The most current self-report data point, and METR is
explicit about why to distrust it.

## Key findings

- Median self-reported **value** uplift: 1.4–2x.
- Median self-reported **speed** uplift: 3x (METR expects speed > value; respondents
  substitute into lower-value-but-faster tasks).
- Retrospective: 1.3x (March 2025) → 2x (March 2026) → forecast 2.5x (March 2027).
- Median willingness-to-sacrifice-salary to keep AI: 29% of salary for one month.
- METR staff report the *lowest* value uplift of any subgroup — possibly because they
  remember the early-2025 RCT's 40-point perception gap.

## METR's own skepticism (this is the valuable part)

- "Survey results are not necessarily grounded in reality."
- Their early-2025 RCT found people overestimated AI's time effect by ~40 percentage
  points.
- "Public estimates of productivity impacts from surveys have tended to be greater than
  those from field experiments or quasi-experiments."
- They reviewed the 7 respondents claiming ≥10x value uplift: in 2 cases they could see
  public outputs and are "confident" the claims are overstated; 2 more suspicious; only 1
  plausibly real.

## Why this matters

- This is the most current self-report snapshot (early 2026, agentic tools widespread).
- It is explicitly positioned as *not* ground truth — the opposite of vendor surveys that
  headline the multiplier without caveat.
- Distinguishing "value" vs "speed" uplift is itself a methodological contribution: speed
  overstates value because of task substitution.

## Claim typing

- Fact (survey run, methodology): high.
- Performance (people *say* AI gives them 1.4–2x value): high — that's what they say.
- Performance (AI actually gives 1.4–2x value): LOW — METR itself says don't trust the
  magnitude, and self-report diverges from measurement per their own prior RCT.
