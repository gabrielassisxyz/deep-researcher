# Review — claude:opus — Round 99

I read the contract, all nine top-level artifacts, and all fourteen source notes, and I
checked the claims in `synthesis.md`, `evidence.md`, and `decision-guide.md` against the
notes they cite. The dossier's argument is sound and its headline conclusion ("the current
state for your condition is unmeasured; measure it yourself") is the right one on the
evidence it has. The problems are in the layer beneath the argument: the sign convention it
announces it fixed is still inverted in three places, its one `high`-confidence performance
claim counts two self-report instruments as replications of a *measured*-vs-self-report
divergence, and its most on-condition source was never actually read.

## False-Positive Risks

Ranked by how likely I am to be wrong.

- **F14 is the one I am least sure of.** METR's own position is that the estimate is weak evidence of *size*, and they list confounds (the $50/hr pay cut, multi-agent time reporting, task substitution) whose direction I did not work out. My claim is narrower than it may read: not "the dossier is wrong about the sign", but "the dossier asserts a known bias direction in `evidence.md` and unknowability in `synthesis.md`, and those cannot both stand." The fix may well be to keep "uninterpretable" and correct `evidence.md`. I flagged it major because whichever way it resolves, it moves the decision-guide row that matters most to this user.
- **F6 (Horikawa baseline)** assumes the paper reports no human control. I am inferring that from the source note, which describes only before/after metrics on 403 AI commits. If the paper does contain a human comparison, the note is the defect, not the claim — but the note is what the dossier cites, so something still needs fixing.
- **F11 (DORA recency)** rests on my knowledge that DORA publishes annually; I could not verify a newer edition in this session (the search tool was not authorized). If no post-2024 edition exists, this collapses to "log the query that shows you looked", which is minor rather than major.
- **F2** could be argued the other way: one might say DORA's *individual self-report vs org delivery-metric* split is a divergence worth counting even though both are survey-derived. I do not buy it — the delivery items are the same respondents answering different questions — but a reasonable reviewer could.
- **F18** is close to pedantry and I nearly cut it. I kept it because this dossier's entire value proposition is methodological scrupulousness about what counts as "measured".
- I am **confident** about F1, F3, F4, F5, F7, F9, F13 — all are mechanically checkable against the files as they stand, and F7/F9 are contradicted by `fixes.md`, which claims they were already repaired.

## What The Dossier Got Right

Genuinely a lot, and the things it got right are the hard things.

- **It refused the easy answer.** The obvious dossier here is "METR says 19% slower, therefore stop." This one found METR's own retraction, led with it, and reported that the current state is *unmeasured* — a conclusion that is less satisfying and more correct. Leading the README and synthesis with the trajectory rather than the number is exactly right.
- **The population argument is the strongest thing in it.** Separating "AI helps developers" from "AI helps *this* developer" via the junior/senior and lab/field gradients, and then showing that the population with the best evidence of gain is not the user's, is the analytically sharp move and it is properly sourced.
- **Q4 (why the evidence conflicts) is the best section.** Decomposing the apparent contradictions into population × metric × tool era × task realism — and noting that a 26% increase in pull requests is not a 26% reduction in task time — is the kind of reasoning most dossiers skip.
- **It does not oversell the local experiment.** The 30-task protocol in `decision-guide.md` is specific, pre-registers the task list, names the failure mode that broke METR's own follow-up, and states the minimum n. That is a usable instrument, not a gesture.
- **The search ledger is real.** Twenty queries with the hijacked ones written down and named ("HIJACKED by 'Meta' the company"), rejections with reasons, and the second opinion's two finds recorded as *coverage failures of my own discovery* rather than quietly absorbed. That last bit is rare and it is what let me find F3 and F4 at all — the dossier told on itself.
